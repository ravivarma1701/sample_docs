MAHABHARATA: THE GREAT EPIC OF INDIA

Introduction

The Mahabharata is one of the longest and most influential epics in
world literature. Traditionally attributed to Sage Vyasa, it narrates
the story of the Kuru dynasty and the conflict between the Pandavas and
the Kauravas. The epic explores duty, justice, leadership, sacrifice,
loyalty, and the consequences of human actions.

Origins of the Kuru Dynasty

King Shantanu of Hastinapura married Goddess Ganga and had a son named
Devavrata, later known as Bhishma. Bhishma took a lifelong vow of
celibacy to ensure his father’s happiness and the continuation of the
royal lineage.

Birth of the Pandavas and Kauravas

Through divine blessings, Kunti gave birth to Yudhishthira, Bhima, and
Arjuna. Madri gave birth to Nakula and Sahadeva. Together they became
known as the Pandavas.

Dhritarashtra and Gandhari had one hundred sons, led by Duryodhana, who
became known as the Kauravas.

Education and Rivalry

The princes were trained by Guru Dronacharya. Arjuna became the greatest
archer, while Bhima was renowned for strength. Duryodhana grew
increasingly jealous of the Pandavas, leading to intense rivalry.

Draupadi and Indraprastha

Arjuna won Draupadi’s hand in marriage at her swayamvara. The Pandavas
later established the prosperous kingdom of Indraprastha, which became a
symbol of their success.

The Dice Game

Duryodhana and Shakuni invited Yudhishthira to a game of dice. Through
deceit, the Pandavas lost their kingdom, wealth, and freedom. Draupadi
was humiliated in the royal court, creating a moral crisis that shaped
the future war.

Exile

The Pandavas spent thirteen years in exile, including one year in
disguise. During this period they gained wisdom, alliances, and military
strength.

The Kurukshetra War

When peace negotiations failed, the great war of Kurukshetra began. The
conflict lasted eighteen days and involved many of the greatest warriors
of the age.

Bhishma, Drona, Karna, Abhimanyu, Ghatotkacha, and countless others
played pivotal roles in the battle.

The Bhagavad Gita

Before the war, Arjuna hesitated to fight his relatives and teachers.
Lord Krishna, serving as his charioteer, delivered the Bhagavad Gita,
teaching duty, selflessness, devotion, and spiritual wisdom.

Victory and Aftermath

The Pandavas ultimately won the war, but at tremendous cost. Nearly the
entire Kuru lineage was destroyed. Yudhishthira became king and sought
to govern with justice and compassion.

Legacy

The Mahabharata remains one of the most important works of Indian
literature and philosophy. Its stories continue to influence culture,
ethics, leadership, and spirituality across the world.
